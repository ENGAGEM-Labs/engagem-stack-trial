## Palette assignments (the bake-off)

Three concepts, one palette each:
- **Concept A — "Mapped" palette:** #87A7AC blue-grey, #E7B97F amber, #EAE6D8 cream, #E4EDEE pale blue, #677A67 sage accent, #43484D text. Calm, warm, organic-premium.
- **Concept B — Brand evolution:** deep navy family (#001830 to #07344d range, concept's call) + vivid green #18d794 as the working accent + white/near-white surfaces. Modernized, confident, unmistakably ENGAGEM.
- **Concept C — Hybrid:** Mapped palette's warm neutrals and calm surfaces, with vivid green #18d794 as the single action/accent color and charcoal text. Warmth plus brand recognition.

After team review, the winning concept re-renders in the other two palettes for a clean palette-only comparison.

## Hard rules (law, all concepts)

- Three deep concepts, not variations: each fully committed to one direction with one deliberate aesthetic risk.
- No purple gradients, no stock humans, no glassmorphism, no generic SaaS card-grid first impression, no Inter/DM Sans.
- No em-dashes anywhere in rendered copy. Copy verbatim, zero edits.
- Mobile-first, one H1, semantic HTML, server-rendered, CSS-only motion behind prefers-reduced-motion, hero rotation per Panel C spec.
- Impeccable /critique runs on all three before presentation; findings fixed or justified.
- web-design-guidelines gate before push.

## Judging criteria

Three-second clarity for a skeptical owner-operator; distinctiveness (never mistakable for a template); belief ladder served: problem, fit, competence, safety; and which palette makes ENGAGEM feel modern without losing itself.
