# ENGAGEM Home Page — Concept Gallery Brief

**Purpose:** Render distinct visual design concepts of the ENGAGEM home page. This is design exploration only. Copy below is DRAFT (final copy pass pending); design direction is NOT locked by tonight's winner. Nat's design brief remains the authority on final direction.

## The business, in three lines
ENGAGEM is a Moncton, NB marketing, automation, and AI agency with 20 years of history. Positioning: we build the things you can't see: integrated websites that capture leads, workflow automation, outreach systems, and AI where it earns its place. Audience: owner-operated businesses (trades, services, SMEs) that are behind on tech and run on word of mouth. Twenty years is evidence, never identity.

## Page structure to render (first four sections only)
1. **Nav:** wordmark left; items: Strategy, Marketing & Lead Gen, Digital Adoption, AI, Blog; "Let's Talk" as the single nav button.
2. **Hero:** H1 + one supporting sentence + one primary CTA button ("Let's Talk"). The H1 uses the rotating-object device rendered statically for concepts: "ENGAGE your market." Supporting line: "We build the systems you can't see: websites that capture leads, automation that saves hours, and AI where it counts."
3. **Trust marquee:** a slow ribbon of ~10 monochrome placeholder client logos (use neutral geometric placeholder marks, never real brands). Caption: "Trusted by businesses across the Maritimes for 20 years."
4. **Why ENGAGEM:** H2 "Put your business on offense." plus 3 benefit blocks, each a short imperative heading + one sentence:
   - **Reclaim your time.** Automation handles the repetitive work so you run the business instead of the busywork.
   - **Put leads on tap.** Your website and outreach work as one system that fills the pipeline while you work.
   - **Leapfrog competitors.** AI where it earns its place gives you capabilities the shop down the road doesn't have.

Use this copy VERBATIM in every concept. Copy is identical across all ten; only design varies.

## Brand reference (a starting point, not a cage)
Current brand colors: deep teal-navy #07344d, vivid green #18d794, white. Concepts may explore beyond this palette; at least 4 of 10 should stay anchored to it so we can compare in-brand vs departure.

## Hard rules, every concept (non-negotiable)
- No purple-gradient template aesthetics, no stock illustration people, no glassmorphism by default, no generic SaaS card grid as the first impression.
- No em-dashes anywhere in rendered copy.
- Mobile-first: large type, generous white space, thumb-reachable CTA. Every concept must be fully responsive.
- One H1 per page. Semantic HTML. All content server-rendered.
- Motion restrained and CSS-only; everything respects prefers-reduced-motion.
- Each concept commits fully to ONE design direction and takes one deliberate, justifiable aesthetic risk. No hedged middle-ground concepts.

## What we judge
Three-second clarity (a stranger knows what's in it for them), distinctiveness (could not be mistaken for a template), and fit for a skeptical owner-operator audience: confident and modern without being Silicon Valley slick.
